Dr Feras Alasali

Principal Investigator Assistant Professor at The Hashemite University

Dr Feras Alasali serves as the Director of the Renewable Energy Center at the Hashemite University in Jordan. In addition to his leadership role, he is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical Engineering, bringing over six years of experience in optimal and predictive control models for energy storage systems and LV network applications. Feras obtained his Ph.D. in Electrical Power Engineering from the University of Reading in 2019.

His research interests are centered on control models for distributed generation and LV networks, load forecasting, and power protection systems. Currently, Feras is exploring the application of emerging technologies such as machine learning and optimization methods. His work aims to optimally simulate network loads, design protection systems for micro and smart grids, and address various engineering challenges.

Dr Anas Almajali

Co-Investigator Associate Professor at The Hashemite University

Dr. Anas AlMajali Anas AlMajali is currently an Associate Professor at the Department of Computer Engineering - The Hashemite University. He received his PhD and MS degrees in computer engineering from the University of Southern California in Fall 2014 and Fall 2008 respectively. He received his BSc in computer engineering from the University of Jordan in 2005. His main research interest is in computer and computer networks security, smart grid security and cyber-physical systems.
Currently, Dr. AlMajali is a senior IEEE member. He is also the secretary of the Jordan Joint Chapter of the IEEE Computer and Computational Intelligence Societies (CS/CIS) for 2022 and 2023 and a member of the executive committee of the IEEE Jordan Section.

Dr Ali Hayajneh

Co-Investigator Assistant Professor at The Hashemite University

Dr. Ali Hayajneh currently serves as a faculty member in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Hashemite University in Zarqa, Jordan. Dr. Hayajneh earned his BSc and MSc degrees from Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST) in Irbid, Jordan, in 2010 and 2014, respectively. He later obtained his Ph.D. from the University of Leeds in the United Kingdom.

His research has garnered support from the Royal Academy of Engineering through two programs: Transfer Systems through Partnerships (TSP) and Distinguished International Associate (DIA). These programs focus on areas such as smart agriculture, drone-assisted micro-irrigation, and tiny machine learning on edge IoT devices.

Dr. Hayajneh's research interests span a wide range of topics, including drone-assisted wireless communications, public safety communication networks, backscatter communication, deep learning, and power harvesting. He is also deeply involved in the study of stochastic geometry, device-to-device (D2D) and machine-to-machine (M2M) communications, modeling of heterogeneous networks, cognitive radio networks, and cooperative relay networks. Additionally, his work encompasses edge computing and reinforcement learning.
